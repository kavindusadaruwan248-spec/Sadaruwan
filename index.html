T01

Act Your Way to Confidence and Growth

Summary

This talk explains that confidence does not come before action. Instead, confidence grows when we take action regularly and learn from experience. The speaker shares how her mother made a brave decision to leave a difficult family situation and improved her life through consistent action. She also introduces the Hot Shot Rule, a self-coaching method where you imagine someone you admire in your role ask, what they would do differently and take action within 24 hours. The speaker believes that overcoming complacency, reflecting on our goals and acting consistently helps us grow both personally and professionally. The main message is that confidence is built through action not by waiting to feel ready.

My Opinion

I found this talk very inspiring because it shows that confidence is not something people are born with. Instead it develops through practice and taking small steps every day I especially liked the Hot Shot Rule because it is a simple and practical way to improve ourselves. The speaker’s personal stories made the message more meaningful and relatable. This talk taught me that I should stop waiting for the perfect moment and start taking action even when I feel unsure.

Reflection

This talk helped me realise that I sometimes hesitate to do new things because I lack confidence. However, I learned that confidence comes after taking action not before it. In the future, I want to challenge myself to take more initiative in my studies and daily life. I can apply the Hot Shot Rule by asking myself what a successful person would do in my situation and then acting on it. By practising reflection, intention, and action regularly, I believe I can continue to grow improve my skills and become a more confident person.

02. Can the Immune System Help Cure Chronic Diseases?

Summary

This talk explains a new idea called forensic immunology, which studies the immune system to find the hidden causes of chronic diseases. The speaker explains how researchers linked the Epstein–Barr virus (EBV) with multiple sclerosis and how the immune system stores a memory of infections. Using machine learning, scientists hope to identify causes of diseases such as dementia, depression and diabetes, leading to better treatments in the future.

My Opinion

I found this talk very interesting and hopeful. I was surprised to learn that the immune system stores so much information about our health. I believe this research could improve the lives of millions of people suffering from chronic diseases.

Reflective Growth

This presentation helped me understand the importance of scientific research and encouraged me to become more curious about new discoveries. It inspired me to develop critical thinking and lifelong learning.
